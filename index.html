Introduction

Each individual has different preferences that are crucial in the way that they understand and comprehend new information. This is why, in the 1970s, an educator from New Zealand by the name of Neil Fleming created what is now known as the VARK Model (Visual, Auditory, Reading, Kinesthetic). The VARK Model aids in differentiating between different peoples sensory preferences when learning. It focuses on four main types of learning styles, Visual, Auditory, Reading/Writing (Or Literary), or Kinesthetic). This system is used internationally in many different schools to accommodate for each students sensory preferences. This model is effective in using these preferences to help utilize ways to comprehend and grasp new, and difficult information.

 

Defining Each Type of Learning Style:

1-    Visual Learner:

A visual learner is somebody who comprehends new information by having a visual example, representation in front of them. Example: Diagrams, Graphs, Photographs. They utilize their visual sensory skills to comprehend new information.

2-  Auditory Learner:

An auditory learner is someone who comprehends new information by having an example they hear being presented to them. Example: Videos. Audiobooks. They utilize their hearing sensory skills to comprehend new information.

 

3-  Reading (Literary) Learner

A Literary learner is someone who comprehends new information by reading or writing the information presented in front of them. Example Websites, books, journals. They utilize their reading/writing skills to comprehend new information.

4-  Kinesthetic Learner

A Kinesthetic learner is someone who comprehends new information by doing hands on experimentation. Example, working with 3D models, step by step experiments. They rely on their sense of touch to grasp new information

Study Techniques for each Type

-         Visual Learner

The most efficient ways for a Visual Learner to study and comprehend more information is through:

   Visual Aids:

Use diagrams, charts, and graphs to aid in understanding information.

   Mind Mapping:

Create a visual map to assist in organizing ideas.

   Color Coding:

Assign colors to concepts to help process information.

   Highlighting/Underlining:

Highlight or Underline to emphasize key points in text visually.

   Visual Summaries:

Make infographics to recap key points.

 

-         Auditory Learner

The most efficient ways for an Auditory Learner to study and comprehend more information is through:

   Audiobooks:

Listen to audiobooks to aid in understanding information.

   Podcasts:

Explore educational podcasts and discussions.

   Verbal Repetition:

Repeat information out loud to reinforce your understanding.

   Oral Summaries:

Summarize the information verbally

   Voice Notes:

Record key points and listen to review.

 

-         Literary (Reading/Writing) Learner

The most efficient ways for a Literary Learner to study and comprehend more information is through:

   Reading:

Read books, articles, and written materials about the material.

   Note-taking:

Create detailed written notes about the topic.

   Annotating Texts:

   Write comments and questions about important parts.

   Summarization:

   Write short summaries of information within the material.

   Journaling:

Write a journal to reflect on your learning.

 

-         Kinesthetic Learner

The most efficient ways for a Kinesthetic Learner to study and comprehend more information is through:

   Field Trips:

Visit places related to the topic to help see the information physically

   Physical Manipulatives:

Use a variety of objects to represent ideas

   Hands-On Activities:

Engage in hands-on experiences

   Experiments:

Conduct experiments to comprehend different concepts.

   Interactive Models:

Work with physical models and props
